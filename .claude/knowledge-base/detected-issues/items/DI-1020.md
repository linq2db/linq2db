# DI-1020 — Misaligned indentation in GuidMemberTranslator.TranslateGuildToString

**Severity**: low  **Category**: code-smell  **Status**: open

## Location
- `Source/LinqToDB/Internal/DataProvider/SqlCe/Translation/SqlCeMemberTranslator.cs:352` — method signature at correct indent, opening brace on following line at wrong indent level

## Pattern matched
Localized structural issue: the opening `{` of `TranslateGuildToString` (line 353) is indented at 3 tabs instead of 4, and the `var factory` declaration (line 356) is at 4 tabs instead of 5, while the remaining body lines (357-362) are correctly indented at 5 tabs. The closing `}` (line 363) is at 4 tabs. This is a mixed-indentation block within an otherwise consistently tab-indented file.

## Why it matters
The mismatch makes the method body visually ambiguous — at a glance the opening brace appears to belong to the enclosing `GuidMemberTranslator` class rather than the method. Any future editor auto-indent or "fix whitespace" pass will silently reindent the block, producing a cosmetic-only but noise-heavy diff. The inconsistency also breaks the column-aligned formatting convention the codebase enforces.

## Suggested fix
Re-indent the method body so the opening `{`, `var factory` declaration, and closing `}` all sit at 4 tabs (consistent with a method body inside a nested `protected class` at depth 3):

```csharp
protected override ISqlExpression? TranslateGuildToString(...)
	{
		//"LOWER(CAST({0} AS char(36)))"

		var factory        = translationContext.ExpressionFactory;
		var stringDataType = factory.GetDbDataType(typeof(string)).WithDataType(DataType.Char).WithLength(36);

		var cast  = factory.Cast(guidExpr, stringDataType);
		var lower = factory.ToLower(cast);

		return lower;
	}
```
